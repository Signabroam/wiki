About CIS-3

Name: CIS-3
Signabroam ID: MD3.3
Frequencies 5287, 5363, 5907, 6911, 7032, 7667, 8106, 8514, 8568, 9283 kHz
Status Active
Voice Male, Female
Mode USB, 14/15 Channels (4/5 Groups of 2/3 Channels)
Location Russia
Associated Stations Southern Russian Networks FGKT
Description The CIS-3 also known at the 3 Tone circuit Stripe, is a 3 channel USB signal. It is a 3 channel signal that is used for unknown purpose, the channels have a 60 TPM (Tick Per Seconds) wave like a clock or something similar. The Purpose is unknown... Is it also used on many Southern Russian Networks station as a Channel Marker.

Description below is from Tyler Stamplfi "KA0KA" @SignalPhantom for SignalPhantom.com on YouTube.

Russian idle communication circuit with 'The Stripe' CIS three-tone seen throughout several Russian military remote Automated Communication Systems (ACS) HF circuits from 06/2023.
The artifact I believed to be part of digital jitter circuit noise or other cause, not directly pointing to any so-called 'network' of shared circuits with the same visible artifact, which can be identified on several active Russian CIS related equipment such as music-loop networks 7032 kHz with associated transmitters and The Buzzer UVB-76, during standby CIS-12 audio transmissions.
The three tones are produced at ~ 2883, 3129, 3363 Hz with a + 5dB higher amplitude for the middle tone with a notable timing pulse in regular intervals, higher FFT analysis shows tones do not align with AT-3104 modem manufacturer by JSC Almaz CIS-12 modes.
